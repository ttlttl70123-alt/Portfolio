ROLE

This academic project was completed during my junior year of university. At the time, South Korea faced a severe shortage of electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure. For domestic travel, Koreans frequently drive to Gangwon Province, a trip that typically takes three to four hours from Seoul. However, once outside the metropolitan area, EV facilities were nearly nonexistent, leaving drivers at risk of being stranded if they ran low on battery mid-journey.

To address this, I designed a Tesla charging station and store in Gangwon Province. By repurposing an existing gas station, the project symbolized the generational shift from internal combustion engines to EVs. Rather than creating a conventional, sales-driven showroom, I designed the space to allow visitors to naturally encounter and experience the products. This approach went beyond simple brand promotion, acting as a direct appeal for the EV system itself.

Furthermore, unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, which are inherently difficult to keep clean during maintenance due to engine oil and other mechanical fluids, EVs allow for a much cleaner service environment. To maximize this advantage, I integrated a functional maintenance area and designed the spatial layout so that this pristine process is fully visible from anywhere within the store. This transparency was intended to leave a strong, positive impression on consumers.
